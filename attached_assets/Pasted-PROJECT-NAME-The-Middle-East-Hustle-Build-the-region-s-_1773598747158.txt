PROJECT NAME

The Middle East Hustle
Build the region’s leading polling and opinion platform

MASTER PROMPT

Design and build a world class digital platform for The Middle East Hustle that transforms an existing profile based media concept into the Middle East’s most influential polling, sentiment, and discovery platform.

This platform should not feel like a news site, a blog, a survey tool, or a betting platform.
It should feel like the daily pulse of the Middle East.
It should be premium, fast, addictive, highly shareable, editorially credible, and culturally relevant across the region.

The platform starts from an existing library of curated Middle East profiles and evolves into a high frequency polling ecosystem where people across the region vote daily on business, ambition, culture, technology, cities, identity, leadership, and the future of the region.

Do not design this as a generic media website.
Do not design this as a cheap viral platform.
Do not design this as a traditional newspaper.
Design it as a category defining media x product x data platform.

CORE STRATEGIC IDEA

The Middle East Hustle already has a foundation: real profiles of influential and emerging regional voices across sectors.
These profiles should become the first community layer of the platform.

The product should combine three engines:

1. Profiles
A premium library of curated people from across the region

2. Polls
Daily high signal, highly shareable questions across categories

3. Network effects
Each profile becomes a distribution node into their own network
Each poll becomes a piece of shareable media
Each vote becomes part of a living regional sentiment dataset

The long term goal is to disrupt media by shifting users from passive content consumption into active participation and daily voting.

PLATFORM POSITIONING

The Middle East Hustle should become:

The daily pulse of the Middle East

A living archive of who matters in the region

This is not about politics as the core driver.
This is about relevance, identity, ambition, culture, work, leadership, innovation, social shifts, and the future of the region.
